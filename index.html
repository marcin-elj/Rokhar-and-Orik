Rokhar and Orik

In a world where every spell costs a memory, a boy and a shapeshifter forge a bond that everyone else wants to break.

What are you willing to forget?

The story

Rokhar is fifteen and carries a secret he doesn't know — at eleven, he accidentally killed his own parents. His magic burned the memory to protect him.

Raised by Veran, an old mage in hiding, he learns a craft fueled by the only currency that matters: memories. Small ones buy small spells. The ones that made you who you are? Those reshape reality.

When a comet crosses the sky and a strange, small creature named Orik appears, everything changes. Their bond is instant, deep, and dangerous — because Orik is the last of an ancient race. And there are people willing to do terrible things to claim what he is.

As a sealed demon stirs beneath the earth and a cult works to set it free, Rokhar discovers that the bond he would die for is exactly what his enemies need to break.

The magic

Every spell requires a memory.
The stronger the memory, the stronger the spell.
What you forget is gone forever.

Two paths. One gives willingly. The other takes by force. Both cost everything.

Some mages sacrifice the smell of their mother's cooking for a spark of fire. Others burn the face of a loved one for the power to level a building. The greatest mages are the emptiest people you'll ever meet.

But Rokhar has something no other mage has — a bond with Orik that protects his most precious memories. A safe harbour in a mind being slowly hollowed out by magic.

Until someone tries to break that bond.

The characters

Rokhar

The boy who forgot

Fifteen. Stubborn. Haunted by something he can't remember. His magic grows stronger with every memory he burns — and weaker with every part of himself he loses.

Orik

The shapeshifter

He arrived with the comet. Small, strange, and full of secrets he doesn't know he carries. Wolf. Bear. Something fiercer. He grows with the bond — and the bond is everything.

Veran

The old mage

Mentor. Protector. The closest thing Rokhar has to a father. He knows things about Rokhar's past that he's never shared. His last words will echo through the entire story.
